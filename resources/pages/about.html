About TrailHead

Trailhead is your own personal conference scheduler. To make it easier for you to find presentations that you care about you can search for people or topics and browse connections between articles. Whatever you find interesting you can add to your own schedule and download it as a pdf or add it to your calendar.

So dive in and click away! Search for a topic you like and use the graph to browse between related talks. Any time you find something that interesting, go ahead and add it to your schedule, to get your own personal exquisite conference program.

Should you later decide to add or drop a few talks, despair not. Trailhead let you save your schedule to come back later. All you need is to copy the link at the top right corner.

Get Started

Graph Legend

  • An article
  • An article you have already added to your schedule
  • The last article you selected
  • An article matching your search term

Navigation

  • Move by dragging the graph with your mouse
  • Zoom by scrolling in or out
  • Add an article to your schedule
  • Remove an article from your schedule

Schedule

  • Begin by clicking on an article in the graph and add it to your schedule
  • Download your schedule as a PDF ready to print
  • Remove all articles from your schedule

Other use

Search: Trailhead let you work with advances search features. You can select more crierions to search nodes in the graph. The filters are saved in the panel and you can select and unselect them.

Schedule: When you're happy witht the schedule you have built in the panel, you can either download a pdf with all the information about the talks you selected, or an ical file. An ical file let you add events in your favorite calendar app. If you want to sync it with your iphone you should follow these steps:

  1. Create a new calendar on your computer that sync with your iphone.
  2. Add the ical events on this calendar.
  3. Connect your iphone and sync.

      4. Or you can also simply send the ical file by mail on open the file on your iphone mail app. If you later make some changes, you can easily remove the old schedule in your calendar and add the new dates and times to your calendar

      How does it work?

      The graph you are looking at is generated by comparing the closeness of a bit more than 600 papers submitted to the 2012 ISIT in Boston. They are currently rated based on a naive bayesian textual comparison of the similarity of the word frequences of the submittet research.

      This project is developed with the help of a series of great tools. The graph is based on D3, a javascript library for creating SVG graphics. The javascript interface is based on Curl, Radio and many more. Check the code out on Github for a full overview.
